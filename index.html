The Journey

From cricket fields in Bengaluru to building AI systems in Los Angeles

The Spark

Growing up in Bengaluru, India's Silicon Valley, I was surrounded by technology and innovation. But it was cricket—India's obsession—that first taught me about strategy, patterns, and data. Watching matches, I'd notice how certain bowlers struggled against specific batsmen, how field placements could change game outcomes.

This fascination led me to wonder: What if we could quantify cricket intelligence? What if AI could analyze decades of matches to find patterns invisible to the human eye? That question became my north star.

From Pixels to Plow: Agricultural Yield Prediction

Deep Learning Framework Using MODIS Satellite Imagery and LSTM Networks

Developed a comprehensive deep learning framework that leverages 12 years of MODIS (Moderate Resolution Imaging Spectroradiometer) satellite imagery to predict agricultural yields in Karnataka, India. The system combines time-series analysis with LSTM networks to forecast NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index) patterns, enabling farmers to optimize crop selection and cultivation strategies based on historical vegetation data and predicted environmental conditions.

Technical Achievements

MODIS Data Processing

Processed 12 years of satellite imagery (144 monthly datasets) covering entire Karnataka region with 1km² spatial resolution and 30-day temporal intervals.

LSTM Architecture

Three sequential LSTM layers with tanh activation achieving R²=0.78 on stress prediction with 12-month time-steps for temporal pattern recognition.

NDVI-Based Forecasting

Calculates Normalized Difference Vegetation Index from NIR and Red spectra, scaled to 10,000 to -10,000 for enhanced interpretability of vegetation health.

Multi-Crop Optimization

Analyzed 5 major crops (rice, corn, jowar, areca nut, ragi) with NDVI thresholds matched to specific growth stages for personalized crop recommendations.

0.12 RMSE Achievement

Technology Stack

Agricultural Impact

This research addresses a critical need for data-driven agricultural planning in Karnataka, where farming is the primary livelihood for millions. By predicting vegetation indices 12 months in advance, the system enables farmers to make informed decisions about crop selection, planting schedules, and resource allocation. The framework demonstrated practical applicability with a case study in Yelandur Taluk, generating a year-round crop rotation strategy optimized for local NDVI patterns.
