The Journey

From cricket fields in Bengaluru to building AI systems in Los Angeles

The Spark

Growing up in Bengaluru, India's Silicon Valley, I was surrounded by technology and innovation. But it was cricket—India's obsession—that first taught me about strategy, patterns, and data. Watching matches, I'd notice how certain bowlers struggled against specific batsmen, how field placements could change game outcomes.

This fascination led me to wonder: What if we could quantify cricket intelligence? What if AI could analyze decades of matches to find patterns invisible to the human eye? That question became my north star.

"Every complex problem is just an unsolved puzzle waiting for the right algorithm."

Building at Scale

At PES University, I dove deep into computer science, but theory wasn't enough. I needed to build. My breakthrough came with the Cricket Analytics Recommender System—the first BERT-based NLP system that could analyze opponent weaknesses by processing years of commentary and statistics. Published in Springer, it validated my belief that AI could revolutionize sports strategy.

Then came Cisco. Suddenly, I wasn't just building projects—I was managing a $5 billion service serving half a billion devices. The stakes were real. I led the modernization of legacy systems to cloud-native architecture, built APIs from scratch, optimized SQL queries from 2 hours to 1 minute, and developed Gen-AI tools that could query across three different database systems seamlessly.

$5B Annual Revenue Managed
500M+ Devices Served
120x Query Performance Boost
96% Test Coverage (AI Tool)

What Drives Me

I'm obsessed with three things: AI that solves real problems, systems that scale, and code that's elegant. Whether it's using LIDAR data for autonomous vehicles at Mercedes Benz, building distributed messaging systems from scratch, or applying deep learning to agricultural yield prediction—I look for challenges that push boundaries.

Now at USC pursuing my Master's in AI, I'm diving deeper into the theoretical foundations while keeping my hands dirty with practical implementations. My goal isn't just to use AI—it's to advance it. To build systems that don't just work, but work intelligently, efficiently, and at scale.

My Philosophy: The best engineers don't just solve today's problems—they build systems that make tomorrow's problems easier to solve.

What's Next

I'm looking for opportunities where I can work on cutting-edge AI/ML problems—whether that's computer vision for autonomous systems, NLP for understanding human behavior, or distributed systems that handle billions of requests. I want to work with teams that value both innovation and execution, where "impossible" is just the starting point.

I believe the next decade of AI won't be about making AI smarter—it'll be about making it more practical, more accessible, and more deeply integrated into systems that touch billions of lives. I want to be at the forefront of that transformation.

Interests beyond code: When I'm not building systems, you'll find me swimming, hiking, exploring LA's food scene, or diving into the world of cinema.
